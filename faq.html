Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our wellness services

Browse by Category

What is Campus Wellness Hub?

Campus Wellness Hub is a comprehensive wellness center dedicated to supporting the physical, mental, and emotional health of university students. We offer a wide range of programs, resources, and services including fitness challenges, nutrition guidance, mental health support, addiction recovery services, and sleep hygiene education.

Are your services free for students?

Yes! All Campus Wellness Hub programs and services are completely free for enrolled university students. This includes individual counseling, group programs, workshops, fitness classes, and access to our resource library.

Where are you located on campus?

We're located in the Student Health Center, Room 200. Our hours are Monday-Friday 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM, and Saturday 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM. We're closed on Sundays, but emergency support is always available.

How do I join a fitness challenge?

You can join fitness challenges through our website or by visiting our office. Simply navigate to the Fitness Challenges page, choose a challenge that fits your level, and click "Join Challenge." You'll receive a welcome email with all the details and access to our tracking tools.

Do I need to have experience to join wellness programs?

Not at all! Our programs are designed for all experience levels. We have beginner-friendly options for every program area, and our staff will help you find the right starting point for your current fitness level and wellness goals.

Can I participate in multiple programs at once?

Absolutely! We encourage students to engage with multiple aspects of wellness. You can participate in a fitness challenge while also attending nutrition workshops and mental health support groups. Our staff can help you create a balanced wellness plan.

Is counseling really confidential?

Yes, all mental health services are strictly confidential. We follow professional ethical guidelines and legal requirements to protect your privacy. Information is only shared with your written consent or in rare cases where there's imminent danger to yourself or others.

What if I'm having a mental health crisis?

If you're experiencing a mental health crisis, please call our 24/7 crisis line at (555) 123-4567, text HOME to 741741, or call 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. You can also visit the nearest emergency room or call 911 if you're in immediate danger.

How long can I receive counseling services?

There's no set limit on counseling services. We work with you to determine the appropriate length of treatment based on your needs and goals. Some students benefit from short-term support, while others engage in longer-term counseling relationships.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule appointments through our website, by calling (555) 123-4567, or by visiting our office in person. We offer same-day appointments for urgent concerns and typically have availability within 2-3 days for routine appointments.

What should I expect during my first appointment?

Your first appointment will include an intake assessment where we'll discuss your wellness goals, current concerns, and health history. This helps us understand how to best support you and develop a personalized wellness plan. The appointment typically lasts 60-90 minutes.

Can I cancel or reschedule my appointment?

Yes, we understand that student schedules can be unpredictable. Please give us at least 24 hours notice when possible by calling our office or using your online account. This helps us offer the slot to another student who may need it.

Will my parents be notified about my use of services?

No, we do not contact parents or guardians about your participation in our programs unless you specifically request this or provide written consent. Students 18 and older have full confidentiality rights regarding their wellness and healthcare information.

Are my records kept separate from academic records?

Yes, all wellness and health records are maintained separately from your academic records and are not accessible to faculty, staff, or administration without your written consent. We follow strict HIPAA guidelines to protect your health information.

What constitutes a wellness emergency?

A wellness emergency includes thoughts of suicide or self-harm, severe panic attacks, substance overdose, eating disorder crisis, or any situation where you feel unsafe or out of control. When in doubt, reach out - we're here to help assess the situation and provide appropriate support.

What happens if I call the crisis line?

When you call our crisis line, you'll speak immediately with a trained crisis counselor who will assess your situation, provide immediate support, and help determine next steps. This might include safety planning, emergency counseling, or connecting you with additional resources.

Do you serve graduate students and staff?

Our primary focus is undergraduate students, but we do offer services to graduate students. University staff should contact Employee Assistance Programs for services. However, staff are welcome to attend our open wellness workshops and events.

Are there specific programs for international students?

While all our programs are open to international students, we also offer specialized support groups and workshops that address unique challenges faced by international students, including cultural adjustment, homesickness, and navigating healthcare systems.

How can I stay updated on new programs and events?

You can subscribe to our newsletter, follow us on social media, or check our website regularly. We also send personalized program recommendations based on your interests and previous participation. You can update your communication preferences in your online account.
